Bachelor of Innovation Degree

Summary

The Bachelor of Innovation (BI) program at The University of Colorado Colorado Springs (UCCS) is a fully accredited alternative to traditional Bachelor of Arts and Bachelor of Science programs. The BI program combines traditional undergraduate coursework in Business, Anthropology, Chemistry, Communication, Computer Science, Data Analytics, and more with practical coursework, teamwork, innovation, and entrepreneurship training. Innovation is at the center of each program inviting students to work in teams to transform ideas into impact. In a dynamic industry, innovation within a business is deemed important to improve productivity, increase competitiveness, improve brand value, and form new partnerships. As part of the requirement to obtain a Bachelor’s of Innovation degree, students must complete three years of team-based consulting work, affectionately referred to as the INOV Team courses. In these courses, students work closely with a client to complete a project that will further the development of the client’s business. Potential clients may apply to work with a student consulting group via the Innovation Program webpage located on the UCCS website. From there, clients are chosen by a selection team via a rolling application process. The BI program grants students the opportunity to work on real problems while local companies are offered the space to work with young professionals in a university setting.
